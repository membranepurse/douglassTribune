Frederick Douglass

American social reformer, abolitionist, orator, writer, and statesman
Frederick Douglass in 1876, around 58 years of age
Accomplishments
  • Narrative of the Life of Frederick Douglass, an American Slave, which became a bestseller
  • Douglass became the first African American nominated for Vice President of the United States
  • Douglass publishing his first abolitionist newspaper, the North Star

"Douglass made a career of agitating the American conscience. He spoke and wrote on behalf of a variety of reform causes: women's rights, temperance, peace, land reform, free public education, and the abolition of capital punishment."

-- Roy Finkenbine, historian
